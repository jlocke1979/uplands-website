Fall in the neighborhood

Light the porches.
Welcome the monsters.

Old houses, glowing sidewalks, and neighbors in the streets make Halloween one of the Uplands’ most joyful informal traditions.

Turn on a porch light. Drive slowly. Say hello to a neighbor.

No committee required

A tradition that already belongs to everyone.

This page celebrates what residents already create. It does not promise an association-run event, publish participating addresses, or require a new fundraiser.

For neighbors

Welcome families

A lit porch can signal participation. Decorations and costumes are optional; a friendly hello is enough.

For drivers

Expect busy sidewalks

Slow down, stay alert, and give families extra space on neighborhood streets.

For newcomers

Join in your own way

Hand out treats, take an evening walk, or simply enjoy the neighborhood energy.

Ideas for a future volunteer team

More magic—only if someone wants to own it.

Possible future additions include a decorated-porch tradition, costume stroll, welcome station, food drive, or neighborhood-history walk.
