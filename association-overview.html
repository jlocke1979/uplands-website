Association Overview

A quick guide to how the Uplands Residential Association works, how neighbors support it, and where that support goes throughout the year.

How the Uplands Works

The Uplands Residential Association is a volunteer-led neighborhood organization that supports community services, neighborhood improvements, communications, and events.

Residents gather at monthly meetings, serve on committees, and help with projects ranging from snow removal coordination to boulevard gardening. The association exists to keep the neighborhood connected and cared for — not to replace city services or manage individual properties.

How the Association is Funded

The URA currently depends on voluntary neighbor contributions. Recurring annual support is the most useful form because it creates a predictable base for planning before seasonal needs arrive.

  • Steady annual support — helps cover communications, administration, neighborhood activities, and advance planning.
  • Snow Fund — supports neighborhood snow removal during the winter season.
  • Beautification Fund — supports boulevard gardening, landscaping, restoration, and beautification efforts.

Confirmed service costs, fundraising goals, and suggested amounts should be published only after board review. See the Support page to choose recurring or one-time giving.

Could a City Funding Tool Provide More Stability?

Neighbors have discussed whether a city-related funding mechanism could provide a more dependable source for shared improvements or services. No proposal, boundary, tax rate, service list, or vote is currently before the neighborhood.

Special Service Area (SSA)

An SSA is a defined area where additional services or improvements are funded through an added property tax. Peoria describes SSAs as a tool for projects such as lighting, sidewalks, and street improvements and currently describes an 80% homeowner / 20% City cost split for its infrastructure model.

Whether boulevard landscaping or contracted sidewalk snow clearing would qualify requires direct confirmation from the City.

Read Peoria’s SSA overview →

Tax Increment Financing (TIF)

TIF is a separate redevelopment-financing mechanism tied to growth in property-tax revenue inside a designated redevelopment district. It is not interchangeable with an SSA and should not be presented as the neighborhood’s plan without substantial City review.

Read Peoria’s TIF overview →

A responsible discussion would require

  1. A clearly defined service problem and verified annual cost
  2. City guidance on eligible mechanisms and legal process
  3. Plain-language estimates of household impact
  4. Broad notice through the Uplander and other channels
  5. Open discussion before any formal proposal or decision

Neighborhood Decisions

Most association business is handled through meetings and ordinary volunteer coordination. Occasionally, a larger question needs broader neighborhood attention.

The Uplander should remain the primary notice channel. The website can provide a stable background page containing the question, verified facts, documents, meeting information, and eventual outcome. A time-limited survey or response form should be added only when the board defines who may respond, how responses will be counted, and whether the result is advisory or binding.
